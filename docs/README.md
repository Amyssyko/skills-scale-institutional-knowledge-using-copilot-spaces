# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This README provides a brief summary of the project management processes used by OctoAcme and links to all process documents in this folder.

## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative project management methodology that emphasizes customer value, clear ownership, and continuous improvement. Projects progress through well-defined lifecycle stages—from initiation and planning through execution, release, and retrospective—ensuring consistent delivery and accountability at every stage.

Team roles and responsibilities are clearly defined: Project Managers coordinate delivery and manage risks, Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize the backlog, and Developers implement and test features collaboratively. Communication cadences are standardized, with daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates keeping everyone aligned.

Quality assurance is built into every phase through automated CI pipelines, code review requirements, and structured acceptance criteria. Each release follows a documented deployment checklist with rollback procedures, ensuring reliability and observability in production.

Continuous improvement is a core principle at OctoAcme. After every sprint, release, or incident, the team runs retrospectives to capture learnings and convert them into actionable backlog items—driving ongoing refinement of processes, workflows, and team practices.

## Key Roles

The following roles are central to OctoAcme project management (see [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) for full details):

- **Project Manager (PM)**: Coordinates delivery, manages schedules, risks, and stakeholder communications.
- **Product Manager (PdM)**: Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures product success.
- **Developers**: Implement features, write and maintain tests, and collaborate on technical design.
- **QA/Testing**: Validate quality and acceptance criteria across all releases.
- **Stakeholders**: Provide inputs, approvals, and business direction.
